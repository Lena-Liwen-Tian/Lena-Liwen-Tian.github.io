Portfolio Dynamics and the Supply of Safe Securities [Internet Appendix]

QCGBF Young Economist Prize, Finalist
FMCG Best Paper Award in Banking/Financial Institution

Modern financial institutions create long-term safe securities by dynamically replacing deteriorated loans in the underlying portfolios. I characterize the market equilibrium of such dynamic collateral management, endogenizing institutions' assets, liabilities, and secondary market trading counterparties.

What Do Lead Banks Learn from Leveraged Loan Investors? (with Max Bruche and Ralf R. Meisenzahl)

In syndicated loan deals, lead banks set price \(P=E[M\cdot C|X,Z]\) by extracting investors' information (\(Z\)) via bookbuilding. Literature implies that \(Z\) is only about investor preferences \((M)\). We find that \(Z\), proxied by "pricing flex" during bookbuilding, is also highly informative about borrower's cash flows \((C)\).
